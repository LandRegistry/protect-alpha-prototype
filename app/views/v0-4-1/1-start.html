Update your title deed

HM Land Registry can warn you by letter, email or text if something changes on your title deed (the record that shows you own your property). A change to your title deed can be the first warning sign of fraud, so it's important to keep your contact details updated.

Use this service to check and update the contact details on your title deed.

Before you start

When you need to change your contact details

You must change the contact details on your title deed if you:

  • move house
  • change your email or phone number
  • are a landlord (letters about your property should not go to your tenants)
  • live in a different property
! Warning If HM Land Registry does not know your contact details, we cannot contact you if something changes on your title deed.

HM Land Registry and online title deeds

Most property owners think that their conveyancer keeps their title deeds, but these are actually just copies. The title deeds are now kept online by HM Land Registry.

HM Land Registry keeps a record of everyone who owns a property (and their land). This record (your title deed) proves that you are the owner.

If you own a house, flat or any other type of property, HM Land Registry has a record of your title deed. This includes your:

  • name
  • address (the one you own)

How this service works

This service lets you check and update the contact details on your title deed.

HM Land Registry recommends that you add an email or phone number to your title deed. These are the quickest way to contact you if there is a problem with your property.

You will:

  • search for your property (by postcode or title number)
  • confirm your address (or addresses, if you own more than 1 property)
  • check your contact details
  • update these details
Adding a postal address to your contact details will not change the address you own.
