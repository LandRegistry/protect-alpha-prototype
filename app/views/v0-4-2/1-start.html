Tell HM Land Registry about a change to your contact details

Use this service to change the address, phone and email on your property record (title deed).

You need to do this if you own land or property but you:

  • have not updated your contact details since you bought your property
  • do not live there
  • rent out property as a landlord
  • live overseas
  • own more than one property
  • change your name
  • get married, divorced or separate from your partner
  • move house
  • remortgage or sell your property
  • have lasting power or attorney

This helps protect your property from fraud, because HM Land Registry will be able to warn you if a fraudster tries to sell your property.

Start now

Before you start

You need the postcode for your property to find your title deed.

If you don't know the postcode, use the title number (your conveyancer will have this).

If you share ownership of your property with somebody else, for example a partner, the other owner will need to change their contact details separately.

When you tell HM Land Registry about a change to your contact details, for example a new postal address, HM Land Registry will wait 15 days before updating your property record.

It takes 15 days because HM Land Registry must make sure that you are the owner before they update your property record.

Once you have submitted your new contact details, HM Land Registry will send you 2 letters to your old and new postal addresses.

These letters are to confirm that your contact details:

  • will change in 15 days
  • have been changed and your property record updated

Once your property record has been updated, HM Land Registry will stop using your 'old' address, email or phone number to contact you.

You don't need to use the online service to update your contact details. You can also visit your nearest HM Land Registry office or send a form by post or email.

Apply by visiting a HM Land Registry regional office

Start by finding your nearest HM Land Registry Office.

When you visit your nearest office, bring:

  • 1 passport photo
  • a scan, photograph or photocopy of:
    • your passport or driving license
    • 2 bills for your property, for example a utility or council tax bill

A member of staff at HM Land Registry will complete the form with you and make copies of your identification.

Apply by post or email

Print the COG 1 form and fill it out.

When you return this form, include:

  • 1 passport photo
  • a scan, photograph or photocopy of:
    • your passport or driving license
    • 2 bills for your property, for example a utility or council tax bill

Post or email the completed form, passport photo and copies of your identification to HM Land Registry:
