Property records: update your contact details

Use this service to update the address, phone and email on your property record. This helps protect your property from fraud, because HM Land Registry will be able to warn you if a fraudster tries to sell your property.

You need to do this if you own land or property but you:

  • have not updated your property record since you bought your property
  • do not live there
  • rent out property as a landlord
  • live overseas
  • own more than one property
Start now

Before you start

You will need a postcode or title number for your property. If you do not know your title number, ask your conveyancer.

If you share your property with somebody else, for example a partner, your partner will need to update their contact details separately.

If you own more than 1 property, you can:

  • use 1 postal address, email or phone number for all your properties
  • update you contact details for all your properties at the same time

If you own more than 1 property, search by postcode or title number and then choose 'add another property' or 'add another title number'.

Update your contact details without using the online service

You don't need to use the online service to update your contact details. You can also do this by:

  • visiting a HM Land Registry regional office
  • sending a form
  • emailing a form

Update your contact details by visiting a HM Land Registry regional office

Find your nearest HM Land Registry Office

When you visit your nearest office, bring:

  • 1 passport photo
  • a scan, photograph or photocopy of:
    • your passport or driving license
    • 2 bills for your property, for example a utility or council tax bill

A member of staff at HM Land Registry will complete the form with you and make copies of your identification.

Update your contact details by post or email

Print this form and fill it out.

When you return this form, include:

  • 1 passport photo
