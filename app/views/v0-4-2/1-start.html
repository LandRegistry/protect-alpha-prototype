Property records: update your contact details

Use this service to check and update your address, phone and email on your property record (title deed).

You need to do this if you own land or property but you:

  • have not updated your property record since you bought your property
  • do not live there
  • rent out property as a landlord
  • live overseas
  • own more than one property

This helps protext your property against fraud because HM Land Registry will be able to contact you if a fraudster tries to buy or sell your property.

Start now

Before you start

You will need a postcode or a title number for your property to use this service. If you do not know your title number, ask your conveyancer.

If you share your property with somebody else, for example a partner, your partner will need to update their contact details separately.

If you own more than one property, you can update contact details for all your properties at the same time. To do this, choose ‘Add another property’ when you search by postcode or title number.

Update your contact details by post, email or by visiting a HM Land Registry regional office

You can also update your contact details by post, email or by visiting a HM Land Registry regional office.

Update your contact details by visiting a HM Land Registry regional office

Find your nearest HM Land Registry Office

When you visit your nearest office, bring:

  • 1 passport photo
  • a scan, photograph or photocopy of:
    • your passport or driving license
    • 2 bills for your property, for example a utility or council tax bill

A member of staff at HM Land Registry will complete the form with you and make copies of your identification.
