You can take steps to protect your house, flat or commercial property from fraud.

This page explains how to:

  • check your property record (title deed)
  • update your contact details
  • sign up for fraud alerts

Check your property record (£3)

HM Land Registry keeps a record of everyone who owns a property (title deeds). These records are kept online.

Property fraudsters will often try to change the name on your property record. If they change the name, they can sell your property.

Check your property record (this costs Â£3) to make sure you are named as the registered owner.

If your name is not on the record, contact HM Land Registry: support@landregistry.gov.uk

Update the contact details on your property record (free)

HM Land Registry will contact you by letter, email or text if something changes on your property record. A change to your property record is usually the first sign of fraud.

If you do not keep your contact details up to date, HM Land Registry cannot contact you.

You should update your contact details if you:

  • have not checked your title deed (property record) since you bought your property
  • do not live there
  • rent out property as a landlord
  • live overseas
  • own more than one property

Once you have updated your new contact details, HM Land Registry will contact you if something changes on your property record. If something changes, HM Land Registrt will:

  • ask if you made this change
  • block the change (if you tell us it wasn't you)
  • freeze your record (to stop the fraudster trying again)
  • investigate the fraud
  • advise you to sign up for property alerts

Sign up for property alerts

Once you have checked your record and updated your contact details, you can also protect yourself against future fraud attempts.

HM Land Registry has a service called property alerts. Sign up to property alerts and HM Land Registry will send you a text if something changes on your property record.
