Before you start

HM Land Registry keeps a record of everyone who owns a property (and their land). This record (your title deed) proves that you are the owner.

If you own a house, flat or any other type of property, HM Land Registry has a record of your:

  • name
  • address (the one you own)

About this service

If there is a problem with your property, for example fraud, HM Land Registry will send a letter to the address you own.

How the service works

Check the contact details on your title deed. These should be the quickest way to contact you.

HM Land Registry recommends that you add an email or phone number. These are the quickest way to contact you if there is a problem with your property.

You will:

  • search for your property (by postcode or title number)
  • confirm your address (or addresses, if you own more than 1 property)
  • check your contact details
  • update these details, if there is a quicker way for HM Land Registry to contact you
  • email
  • phone number
  • postal address (this can be different to the property you own)
Adding a correspondence address to your title deed will not change the address you own.

Identification documents you will need

If you want to use this service, you must prove your identity as the property owner.

List of accepted identification

You will need 3 documents to prove your identity as the property owner. You can use your:

  • passport
  • driving licence
  • national insurance number
