Before you start

HM Land Registry keeps a record of everyone who owns a property (and their land). This record (your title deed) proves that you own your land.

If you own a house, flat or any other type of property, HM Land Registry has a record of your:

  • name
  • address (you own this property)
  • address for correspondence (you live at this property)

About this service

If there is a problem with your property, for example fraud, HM Land Registry will send a letter to the address you own.

How it works

You will search for the property you own (by postcode). We will show you the contact details we have for this property.

These contact details should be the quickest way to contact you. You can add or change your:

  • email
  • phone number
  • correspondence address
You cannot change your registered title address (property you own).

HM Land Registry recommends that you add an email or phone number. These are the quickest way to contact you if there is a problem with your property.

Identification documents you will need

Want to check and confirm your contact details on your property record? You will need to prove your identity as the property owner.

You will need 3 of the following documents to prove your identity as the property owner:

  • Passport
  • Driving license
  • National insurance number
