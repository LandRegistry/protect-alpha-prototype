What happens next?

Your new contact details have been sent to HM Land Registry (the organisation that looks after property records).

We will send you a letter when we have added your new contact details to the register (this can take up to 2 weeks while we check that you are the registered owner).

If you have added an email or phone number to your contact details, we’ll also send you an email or text. You don’t have to do anything else, the email/text/letter is just to confirm these changes.

HM Land Registry will:

  • check that you own this property
  • approve your new contact details
  • add your (new) contact details to your title deed
  • send you a tbc to confirm this change (plus an email / text, if you added this to your contact details)
  • send fraud alerts to these new contact details if we suspect fraud on your property

We will send confirmation letter (plus email/text) to your:

  • old contact details (the ones on the title register before you changed them)
  • new contact details (the ones you changed today)

What happens if a fraudster tries to change my contact details?

If HM Land Registry suspects that someone is not the owner of a property, we will open a fraud enquiry. We will not publish any new contact details if we suspect fraud.

After your new contact details have been approved, we will use these to contact you if we suspect fraud on your property. We will not keep sending fraud alerts to your ‘old’ contact details.
