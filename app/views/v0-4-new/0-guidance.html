You can take steps to protect your house, flat or commercial property from fraud.

This page explains how to check your online title deed, keep your contact details up to date, and sign up for fraud alerts.

Update the contact details on your title deed

If HM Land Registry has your email, phone number or correspondence address, we can contact you if we suspect fraud on your property.

Check and update the contact details on your title deed (this is a free service). You should keep these details updated every time you move or change your email or phone number.

Check your title deed (£3)

HM Land Registry keeps a record of everyone who owns a property. These records (title deeds) are kept in an online register.

Property fraudsters often try to change the registered owner on a title deed, so they can sell or remortgage the property.

To keep your home safe, check your title deed to make sure you are named as the registered owner.

Check your online title deed (this costs £3). If your name is not on the deed, contact HM Land Registry: support@landregistry.gov.uk

Sign up for property alerts

Once you have checked your title deed and updated your contact details, you can also protect yourself against future fraud attempts.

HM Land Registry has a service called Property alerts. You can sign up to property alerts and we will email you if something changes on your title deed.
